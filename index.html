Oscar Health Covers Online Therapy and Mental Health

All Oscar Health members in New York have mental health coverage. Therapy is treated as an essential health benefit, which means it's covered under every Oscar plan at the same level as your medical care.

Oscar's behavioral health benefits in New York are administered by Optum Behavioral Health. When you search for an in-network therapist with your Oscar plan, you're searching the Optum provider network. Manhattan Mental Health Counseling is in that network.

There's no preset annual session limit. You should be allowed to see an in-network MMHC therapist on a weekly basis as long as your Oscar coverage is active and there is a medically necessary reason for your visits.

Does Oscar Cover Mental Health?

Coverage for therapy varies from one Oscar plan to another. Most plans have a fixed copay per session, with the deductible waived for behavioral health. Some plans do not waive the deductible.

If you have an Oscar Simple Secure plan, your first three sessions are typically $0. If you have an Oscar Chronic Care Plan, behavioral health visits are $0. If you have a Bronze or Silver plan, you may pay coinsurance until your deductible is met.

With an MMHC Oscar therapist, your therapy sessions will likely cost between $0 and $25 per session. The $0-$25 range is the copay (the fixed amount you owe at each visit). For most Oscar Gold and Platinum members, the deductible is waived for psychotherapy. If that is the case, you will be responsible for any applicable Oscar copay.

Why MMHC Is Different for Oscar Members

If you've ever used an insurance directory to find a therapist, you know the pattern. You call ten providers, three call back, two have openings, and you settle for the closest match.

MMHC operates differently. We match you with a clinician based on your concerns, your preferred modality, and your schedule. No waitlist. Pre-screening within days. Same-week therapy available.

If after your first or second session you feel the fit isn't right, we rematch you with a different MMHC clinician. No new intake, no re-verification, no lost progress.

This matters because in virtual therapy specifically, getting the right initial match is more important than in-person therapy. A 2024 systematic review in Clinical Psychology Review found that the therapeutic alliance still predicts treatment outcomes online, but the effect is somewhat weaker than in-person sessions. The clinician you start with carries more weight.

Our Seamless Rematch policy replaces the directory-search model with a supported matching process.

Questions to Ask Oscar to Determine Your Coverage

To know more about the specifics of your plan, call the member services number on the back of your Oscar insurance card. You can also sign in to the Oscar member portal and check your benefits there.

Questions to ask Oscar regarding your mental health coverage:

  • What is my copay for mental health outpatient services, specifically in connection with codes 90834 and 90837? (those CPT codes are used by Oscar therapists)
  • Is there a session cap per year? (session caps are unlikely with Oscar, but ask to know for certain)
  • Is the deductible waived for behavioral health? (for most Oscar Gold and Platinum plans, the deductible is waived, but ask to confirm)
  • Is Manhattan Mental Health Counseling in the Optum Behavioral Health network for my plan?
